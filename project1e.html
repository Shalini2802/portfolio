Coffee Shop Sales

Excel Project

  1. Home
  2. Coffee Shop Sales

Introduction:

This project involved creating a dynamic Excel dashboard to analyze and visualize sales data for a coffee shop. The goal was to uncover business insights such as best-selling products, peak sales periods, and overall performance trends to support data-driven decisions.

Data Preparation:

  1. Data Loading:

    The dataset is loaded into Power Query for further analysis.

  2. Data Type Adjustment:

    Data types are modified to ensure consistency and accuracy in subsequent analyses.

  3. Column Removal:

    The "neighbourhood" column, containing only null values, is removed to streamline the dataset.

  4. Price Formatting:

    The price column is formatted as currency to enhance readability.

  5. Date Extraction:

    Year and month are extracted from the "last review date" column to facilitate temporal analysis.

Pivot Tables:

Pivot tables are created to summarize and visualize key insights:

  1. Hour vs. Quantity Ordered:

    Shows customer demand across different hours of the day. Helps identify peak business times and optimize staffing or promotions.

  2. Weekday vs. Total Sales:

    Provides a breakdown of sales performance across each day of the week to identify the most profitable days.

  3. Store Location vs. Total Sales:

    Displays how each store (e.g., Hell’s Kitchen, Soho, etc.) performs in terms of revenue, helping guide location-specific strategies.

  4. Month vs. Total Sales:

    Visualizes monthly revenue trends to assess seasonality and growth over time.

  5. Product Category vs. Total Sales:

    Highlights which product categories (e.g., Coffee, Bakery, Tea) generate the most revenue.

  6. Top 5 Product Types by Sales

    Ranks individual items (e.g., Barista Espresso, Americano) based on their contribution to sales, helping refine inventory and marketing.

Measures/KPIs:

  1. Total Sales: $6,98,812.33 Total revenue generated from all coffee shop locations.
  2. Total Footfall: 1,49,116 Total number of transactions or customers.
  3. Average Bill per Person: $4.69 Indicates how much an average customer spends.
  4. Average Order per Person: $1.44 Represents the number of items ordered per person.

Pivot Charts:

Pivot charts are generated to visually represent key findings from pivot tables:

  1. Hourly Demand: Line chart showing quantity ordered during each hour of the day — peak between 8 AM and 10 AM.
  2. Sales by Weekday: Bar chart comparing total sales across days — Friday and Sunday perform best.
  3. Store-wise Sales:Column chart showing revenue by store — Hell’s Kitchen leads.
  4. Category-wise Sales: Pie chart visualizing sales share by category — Coffee dominates.
  5. Top 5 Products by Quantity Sold: Bar chart showing the most ordered product types.

Results:

  1. Hourly Demand:

    The majority of sales occur during morning hours (8–10 AM), suggesting strong demand for breakfast items and morning coffee. Ideal for targeted promotions.

  2. Weekly Trends:

    Friday and Sunday are the highest-grossing days, making them key focus points for weekend deals or bulk promotions.

  3. Category Preferences:

    Coffee is the top-selling category by a large margin. Among all, Barista Espresso is the most ordered item.

  4. Monthly Growth:

    Sales steadily increased from January to June, suggesting successful marketing or seasonal growth.
