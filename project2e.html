Call Center Analysis

Excel Project

  1. Home
  2. Call Center Analysis

Introduction:

This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of call center operations, helping stakeholders understand call performance, revenue contribution, agent productivity, customer satisfaction, and demographic engagement. The dashboard aggregates data across locations, representatives, customers, and time dimensions to aid in strategic decision-making.

Data Preparation:

  1. Data Loading:

    The dataset is loaded into Power Query for further analysis.

  2. Data Type Adjustment:

    Data types are modified to ensure consistency and accuracy in subsequent analyses.

  3. Column Removal:

    The "neighbourhood" column, containing only null values, is removed to streamline the dataset.

  4. Price Formatting:

    The price column is formatted as currency to enhance readability.

  5. Date Extraction:

    Year and month are extracted from the "last review date" column to facilitate temporal analysis.

Pivot Tables:

Pivot tables were created to summarize and extract key metrics from call center data:

  1. Representative vs. Total Calls:

    Shows how many calls each agent handled, helping evaluate individual workloads.

  2. Representative vs. Total Amount:

    Highlights revenue generated per representative.

  3. City vs. Total Revenue:

    Compares the performance of Cincinnati, Cleveland, and Columbus in terms of revenue generation.

  4. Customer vs. Revenue per City:

    Breaks down customer-wise contributions across locations.

  5. Day of Week vs. Call Count:

    Reveals which days of the week experience higher call volumes.

  6. Rating Distribution:

    Visualizes how customers rated their experiences.

  7. Total Duration:

    A total of 89,850 minutes spent on calls.

  8. 5-Star Ratings Count:

    307 calls received the highest rating.

  9. Top Performer:

    Representative R03 generated the highest revenue ($20,872).

Measures/KPIs:

  1. Total Calls: 1,000 Total number of calls handled across all customer service representatives.
  2. Total Revenue: $96,623 Total revenue generated through customer interactions.
  3. Average Call Rating: 4 out of 5 Represents the average satisfaction score provided by customers.
  4. Total Duration: 89,850 minutes Cumulative time spent on all calls by representatives.
  5. 5-Star Ratings Count: 307 Number of calls where customers gave the highest satisfaction rating.
  6. Top Performer: Representative R03 Achieved the highest revenue generation with $20,872.

Pivot Charts:

Pivot charts are generated to visually represent key findings from pivot tables:

  1. Total Calls per Month:A line chart shows March and November had peak call volumes.
  2. Total Calls per Weekday: A bar chart highlights Friday as the busiest day, Sunday as the least.
  3. Revenue by Gender & City: A stacked bar chart compares male vs. female contribution in each city.
  4. Customer Ratings Distribution: A column chart showing most ratings are 4 or 5 stars.
  5. Total Calls and Revenue by Representative: Horizontal bar charts display agent performance in terms of count and revenue.

Results:

  1. Representative Performance:

    R03 leads in revenue with $20,872. R02 handled the most calls (218), followed closely by R05 and R01.

  2. Location-Based Revenue:

    Columbus tops the chart with $32,713 in revenue. Cleveland and Cincinnati follow with $37,849 and $26,061 respectively.

  3. Customer Ratings:

    Majority of the calls were rated 4 or 5 stars, indicating high satisfaction. Only a small number of calls had ratings below 3.

  4. Call Volume Trends:

    Fridays are the busiest day of the week, while Sundays are the quietest. Monthly trends show spikes in March and November.

  5. Gender-Based Insights:

    Revenue contribution varies slightly by gender across different cities. Cleveland had a significantly higher female customer revenue.
